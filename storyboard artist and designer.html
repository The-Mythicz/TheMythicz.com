Storyboard Artist

Hyderabad, Telangana, India

Apply

Minimum Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's Degree in Animation, Fine Arts, Graphic Design, Illustration, or related fields.
  • Professional Experience in storyboarding, animation, or illustration, usually 1-3 years for junior positions, or 3-5 years for more senior roles.
  • Previous experience working on game production or interactive media is often preferred.
  • Portfolio showcasing a strong understanding of storytelling, character design, action scenes, and visual composition. Game-related projects or animations are a plus.
  • Proficiency in storyboarding software such as Toon Boom Storyboard Pro, Adobe Animate, or TVPaint. Strong skills in drawing and illustration (traditional or digital), with attention to detail and creativity. Understanding of camera angles, framing, timing, and visual narrative flow. Familiarity with game engines (e.g., Unity, Unreal Engine) is a bonus but not mandatory.
  • Strong ability to visualize and communicate ideas through drawings, with a clear understanding of narrative structure. Ability to interpret scripts and translate them into visual sequences that align with the game’s concept. A good understanding of how gameplay and storytelling interact, especially in interactive media like games.
  • Good communication skills to collaborate with game designers, directors, animators, and other team members. Ability to receive and incorporate feedback, working iteratively on concepts. Strong time management and organizational skills to handle multiple tasks and meet deadlines.
  • Knowledge of game mechanics, game genres, and current trends in the gaming industry is highly valuable. Passion for games and understanding of what makes a good gameplay experience, as storyboards in games often drive interactive narratives.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Master's Degree in Animation, Fine Arts, Graphic Design, Illustration, or related fields.
  • Understanding of 3D modeling or basic 3D software could be beneficial if the game production involves 3D animation or environments. Experience in motion graphics or cinematography is a plus, especially for games with cinematic sequences.

Responsibilities:

  • Collaborating with writers, directors, and game designers to understand the story, character arcs, and gameplay mechanics. Creating visual representations of the game’s key scenes, cinematics, or cutscenes, showing how the story will unfold visually.
  • Drawing scenes that capture the emotions, actions, and transitions between moments in the game. Focusing on the layout, composition, camera angles, and how characters or objects move within the frame.
  • Interpreting the written script into a sequence of images, ensuring the flow and timing of the story or gameplay are clearly represented. Breaking down dialogue, actions, and important moments into clear, concise visual panels.
  • Working closely with concept artists to ensure consistency in visual style and theme. Coordinating with animators to ensure that the storyboard’s visual style is achievable within the technical constraints of the game engine.
  • Considering how game mechanics and interactivity will impact storytelling, particularly in interactive narrative-driven games. Planning the use of gameplay features (like player choices) that may influence the narrative flow.
  • Receiving feedback from directors and other team members and revising the storyboards accordingly to improve the visuals and clarity of the narrative. Iterating on designs based on feedback from the development team to ensure the story fits into the broader game world and mechanics.
  • Using storyboards as a tool to communicate the game’s vision clearly across departments, ensuring everyone is aligned on the visual and narrative direction. Ensuring that the narrative vision aligns with the game’s overall goals and provides clear guidelines for animators, modelers, and level designers.
  • Understanding the capabilities and limitations of the game engine and the production pipeline, ensuring that storyboards are realistic and can be translated into the final game.
